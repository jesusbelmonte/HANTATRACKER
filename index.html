About the Virus

🦠

What Is Hantavirus?

Hantaviruses are a family of RNA viruses carried by rodents. They cause two severe syndromes in humans: Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS) in the Americas and Hemorrhagic Fever with Renal Syndrome (HFRS) in Europe and Asia.

⚠️

How Is It Transmitted?

Primarily through inhalation of dust contaminated with infected rodent droppings, urine or saliva. Person-to-person transmission is rare and limited to the Andes strain in South America. There is no insect vector involved.

🚨

What to Do If Exposed?

Seek immediate medical attention. Early symptoms mimic influenza: fever, fatigue, muscle aches. No specific antiviral therapy exists — ICU-level supportive care is standard. Early intervention significantly improves survival odds.
